The Unclear Solution to Regulate Drag Racing in Queens Community District 2

By: Renee Onque | November 15, 2020

Click the video above to press play. Video provided by Cinthya Lopez, a Sunnyside resident, during a drag racing meet-up in Sunnyside's Home Depot parking lot.

311 requests for drag racing in Queens Community District 2, which includes Sunnyside, Woodside and Long Island City, have skyrocketed.

As the pandemic leaves people in their homes, drag racing tracks in New Jersey continue to cancel events following COVID-19 restrictions, which many people believe are the cause of the increase of drag racing.

Link to Extended Data

"This is a new problem that everybody is talking about. It has never been like this before," Lisa Deller, chair of Community Board 2 told Sunnyside Post.

Sunnyside Post also reported that in a recent statement, the police said they will do more patrols in the area to control this issue.

Yet, Rehndell Masi, a Sunnyside resident, said, "I really don't think that the local government should get the police involved because that's a recipe for disaster."

Although there are varying opinions, something must be done to regulate reckless drag racing before someone ends up hurt.
